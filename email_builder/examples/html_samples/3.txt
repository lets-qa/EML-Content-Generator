        <h2>📡 Public Wi‑Fi: Because Who Doesn’t Love Sharing… Everything?</h2>
        <p>
          Public Wi‑Fi is like free candy from a stranger — tempting, but you might regret it later.  
          Sure, it’s convenient at coffee shops, airports, and that one suspicious laundromat, but it’s also a hacker’s playground.
        </p>
        <h3>Why It’s Risky</h3>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Man-in-the-Middle Attacks:</strong> No, it’s not a wrestling move — it’s when someone intercepts your data mid‑transit.</li>
          <li><strong>Fake Hotspots:</strong> “Free_Airport_WiFi” might actually be “Evil_Hacker_WiFi.”</li>
          <li><strong>Unencrypted Traffic:</strong> If the site isn’t using HTTPS, your data is basically in a postcard format.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Best Practices</h3>
        <p>
          - Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) — it’s like putting your data in a locked suitcase.
          - Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public networks.

          - Turn off file sharing and AirDrop unless you enjoy surprise files from strangers.
        </p>
        <p style="font-size: 12px; color: #888888;">
          P.S. If you see me in a café with 12 open laptops, I’m definitely not hacking… probably.
