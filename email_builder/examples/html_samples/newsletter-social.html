Social Engineering: The Hacker’s Jedi Mind Trick

“These Aren’t the Credentials You’re Looking For”

Social engineering is less about hacking computers and more about hacking people. It’s the art of convincing you to hand over your secrets willingly — like a magician who makes your wallet disappear, except the trick is done over email or phone.

Attackers might pose as IT support, a trusted coworker, or even your boss, using urgency, authority, or fear to get you to click a link, share a password, or approve a suspicious request. It’s basically phishing, but with better acting skills.

Remember: if someone’s asking for sensitive info and you weren’t expecting it, verify through another channel. And if your “CEO” emails you at 3 a.m. asking for gift cards, either they’ve been hacked or they’ve developed a very strange hobby.

💡 Tech Tip of the Day: Always verify requests for sensitive information — preferably in person or via a known, trusted contact method.
